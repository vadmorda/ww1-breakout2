WW1: Breakout

Jacques has managed to overcome the field of barbed wire. He now arrives at a semi-destroyed farmhouse. Cautiously, he investigates the area, wary of any potential enemies. However, he only finds a few cats and a couple of goats. It seems like a safe place to rest before continuing his journey northwest.

Jacques' Rest

Inside a room with a dirty cherry wood floor, Jacques sits down, leaning his back against the wall. He removes his supply bag and rummages inside, pulling out a can of stewed meat and a piece of hard bread. He knows he must find more water. Now, he eats slowly, savoring the moment—it will be his only meal of the day. Later, he plans to rest for two or three hours before heading out under the cover of darkness. It is the safest time to travel through open fields. By dawn, he will have reached the forest, where the thick foliage will offer him protection. As he finishes eating, he lies down on the ground, and memories begin to flood his mind...
